El Fuego had exceptional food but near-zero digital presence. Their DoorDash listing had no professional photography, their Instagram was dormant at 800 followers, and their brand identity was inconsistent across every touchpoint.

Services Delivered
  • Full visual identity system
  • Professional content direction
  • DoorDash listing overhaul
  • Meta Ads campaign (full funnel)
  • Instagram growth management

Timeline

90-day sprint engagement
January – March 2024

The Situation

El Fuego's owner, Marco Delgado, came to us frustrated. He'd invested heavily in his interior, his kitchen, and his team — but his restaurant was invisible online. DoorDash was sending orders to competitors who'd simply done the basics better: better photos, better descriptions, better ratings.

His Instagram had 800 followers after two years of sporadic posts, and there was no cohesive visual identity tying his menus, signage, and social presence together. He was losing to louder brands, not better ones.

"I knew we had the best Latin food in Miami. We just needed people to see it."
— Marco Delgado, Owner

Our Approach

We started with a full visual audit. The brand needed a cohesive identity — warm terracotta tones, a refined wordmark, and a photography style that captured fire, craft, and Latin energy. Week one was brand foundations.

Week two, we overhauled the DoorDash and Uber Eats listings with a new photography brief, restructured menu descriptions optimized for search, and launched a review response protocol to address every existing 3-star review personally.

By week three, we launched a Meta Ads campaign targeting Miami's 25–45 demographic within a 5-mile radius — dinner-focused creative on Instagram and retargeting for anyone who'd visited the website. We ran parallel organic content at a pace of 12 posts per month.

The Results

By day 90, El Fuego was the #1 Latin restaurant on DoorDash within their ZIP code. Delivery orders had increased 218%. Their Instagram grew from 800 to over 62,000 followers — and more importantly, those followers were converting. Meta Ads returned 4.1x on every dollar spent. The dining room went from 40% to 90% occupancy on weekends within two months of launch.

Challenge

Casa Blu was a stunning property with poor photography, a fragmented brand voice, and a Google rating that had dropped to 3.8 stars due to unmanaged negative reviews. Their digital presence did not match the luxury of their physical space.

Services Delivered
  • Luxury brand identity refinement
  • Editorial photography direction
  • Monthly content calendar
  • Google Ads (search + display)
  • Review management system
  • Influencer partnership program
Timeline

6-month retainer engagement
March – August 2024

The Situation

Sofia Reyes, Casa Blu's Director of Marketing, came to us after a difficult season. The property was stunning — lush gardens, a world-class spa, oceanfront suites — but their online presence was communicating none of it. Their Instagram was a graveyard of blurry iPhone photos and missed opportunities.

Worse, three unresolved negative reviews had dragged their Google rating to 3.8 stars — actively costing them bookings from travelers who filtered by rating on travel platforms.

"Luxury travelers decide in 8 seconds whether a property is worth their time. Our digital presence was failing that test."
— Sofia Reyes, Director of Marketing

Our Approach

Month one was about reputation recovery. We responded personally to every negative review, worked with Sofia's team to address the underlying service gaps, and launched a proactive review collection system for satisfied guests — email follow-ups, QR codes at checkout, and staff protocols.

Month two, we delivered a refined brand identity: a color palette that communicated tropical luxury (deep teal, warm sand, brushed gold), a new photography direction brief, and an editorial content framework built around "The Casa Blu Life" — aspirational lifestyle content, not just room photos.

Month three onward: a 3x/week posting cadence, a micro-influencer partnership with 6 Miami-based travel creators, and Google Ads targeting luxury travel search terms — "boutique hotels Miami Beach," "oceanfront resort Florida," and similar high-intent queries.

The Results

Within 6 months, Casa Blu's Google rating climbed from 3.8 to 4.9 stars. Instagram grew to 128,000 followers, and direct bookings attributed to Instagram increased 192%. Google Ads returned 5.2x ROAS on a targeted luxury travel budget. The property was featured organically by two major travel publications after influencer content went viral — reaching over 2M impressions at zero additional cost.

Challenge

Wing Street was a new ghost kitchen concept with no brand recognition, no reviews, and no existing audience. The Chicago delivery market is intensely competitive. They needed to launch, rank, and scale — fast.

Services Delivered
  • Ghost kitchen brand identity
  • DoorDash & Uber Eats launch setup
  • Menu photography direction
  • Review acquisition strategy
  • TikTok Ads campaign
  • Instagram brand building
Timeline

60-day launch sprint
June – July 2024

The Situation

David Park had the kitchen space, the recipe, and the concept — but no idea how to compete on DoorDash against established brands with hundreds of reviews and optimized listings. Ghost kitchens live and die by their platform presence, and Wing Street had none.

He needed more than a listing. He needed a full delivery brand strategy that could generate volume fast enough to climb the algorithmic rankings on DoorDash before his runway ran out.

"The DoorDash algorithm rewards momentum. Our job was to create that momentum before competitors noticed."
— Our Strategy Team

Our Approach

We started with the brand. Ghost kitchens need scroll-stopping names, photography, and descriptions — because the entire purchase decision happens on a phone screen in under 10 seconds. We developed a bold visual identity: high-contrast photography, crave-inducing menu copy, and a brand personality that was unapologetically indulgent.

Launch week, we ran a DoorDash promotional campaign paired with a TikTok Ads push targeting Chicago's 18–35 demographic with short-form video of the product. We also launched a review collection protocol — every completed order triggered a follow-up through DoorDash's messaging system encouraging a review.

By week 6, Wing Street had 300+ reviews averaging 4.8 stars and was ranking #1 in the Chicken/Wings category in the Chicago Loop area — a position they've maintained ever since.

The Results

From launch week to week 8, orders grew 310%. TikTok Ads returned 3.4x ROAS with a relatively modest budget, and the organic content we produced for their Instagram drove an additional 22,000 followers who became repeat customers. Wing Street went from zero to a self-sustaining delivery operation that now consistently outperforms legacy competitors in their category.
